## The problem with "doing all the right things"

Grades, APs, honor societies, lab research, robotics. Every strong applicant has them. Admissions officers read thousands of resumes that look identical. The students who stand out aren't the ones with the longest list — they're the ones who **built something only they could have built.**

It has never been easier to do this. And it has never mattered more.

## What your student will ship

Six weeks. One 90-minute session per week on Zoom. Weekly office hours for drop-in help. By the end, your student will have shipped:

- **A personal website** live on their own domain
- **An interactive app** they can save to their iPhone home screen
- **A data-powered app** pulling real info from the internet
- **A full-stack app** with logins, saved data, and real users
- **An AI-powered app** with Claude built in
- **A native iOS app** running on their own phone — built entirely by them

Real things. Real links. Real apps their friends can actually use.

## The three skills they'll actually learn

Modern building is becoming one job. Ship.Ihsan teaches all three:

- **Product intuition** — how to decide *what* to build
- **Design sense** — how to make it look and feel great
- **Engineering fluency** — how to make it actually work

Plus the meta-skill: **how to get the most out of AI.** Not "AI for homework." AI as a collaborator — the way the best adults in the workforce are learning to use it right now.
